Fishtagged.com FAQ

We are a private group of DMV Potomac River anglers tagging smallmouth bass.

Over the past 5 years we have tagged 1500 plus fish on the upper Potomac from Great Falls to Millville WV and Shepherdstown, MD. Primarily near Seneca and Point of Rocks

So we may better understand growth rates, seasonal migration, range, preferred habitat, predation and the population density.

We use T Tags which include a 1 - 4 digit number unique to that fish. They are 3 inches long and come in various colors. The tag is inserted behind the dorsal fin so it does not adversely affect their day to day movements.

Algae quickly grows on a tag. You may need to gently scrape the tag to read it. The tag may stay in place.

If you wish to look up and see where your tagged fish was originally captured we have a simple and free mapping tool at fishtagged.com which will show the location and hydrological conditions when that fish was tagged and recaptured.. Enter the numbers as they appear (example from the white tag above enter the number only 1085) and then click Zoom. No login or personal information is required.

By submitting all the information to the fishtagged.com form we are able to archive and track the population, growth rate and migratory routes. By correlating river hydrological data we are also able to better understand conditions which dictate movement and feeding vs protective modes. If you choose to share all the info requested below we will send you a photograph of the fish and metadata.

Please submit the data. This is one of the most important elements of archiving data.
